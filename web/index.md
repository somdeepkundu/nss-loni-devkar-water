Loni Deokar sits in the drought-prone eastern edge of Pune district, in the
Bhima / Ujani command. Its farms run almost entirely on a deep, slowly-recharging
aquifer — making **crop choice the village's primary lever against groundwater
depletion**. This site combines the 6th Minor Irrigation Census, a digitised
cadastral survey, and an FAO-56 crop-water model to show where the water goes
and where it could go further.

## Where the water goes

Loni Deokar draws *all* of its irrigation from wells. There is no surface-flow
or lift scheme to fall back on in a poor monsoon.

## Income per drop

Sugarcane dominates the cropping pattern but returns the **least income per
cubic metre** of any local crop. Grapes and onion earn 4–8× more per drop at a
fraction of the water.

<div class="viz"><canvas id="cwpChart"></canvas></div>

!!! tip "The opportunity"
    A gradual shift from sugarcane toward high-value, low-water crops (grapes,
    onion) could **raise farm income and slow aquifer depletion at the same
    time** — the rare win-win in water-stressed agriculture.

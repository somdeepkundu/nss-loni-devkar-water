Loni Deokar sits in the **Ujjaini dam command zone** (Bhima basin, eastern Pune district). Unlike rain-fed villages, it benefits from surface water releases that recharge the upper aquifer—making it **waterlogging-prone during high-release periods** rather than water-scarce. The 2025 La Niña event exposed this: above-normal monsoon + dam releases caused **maize crop failure due to soil waterlogging**, not drought. This site combines the 6th Minor Irrigation Census, a digitised cadastral survey, well-depth observations (red-rock indicators), and the 2025 crop loss data to show where water causes damage and where crop choice can turn water surplus into productivity.

## Why crop zoning works in Ujjaini command

Loni Deokar's irrigation is a **hybrid system**: Ujjaini dam releases create seepage into the upper aquifer during monsoon/post-monsoon; wells supplement during low-release windows. Waterlogging is not a curse—it's a feature to leverage.

## Crop suitability: Water, income, and flood tolerance

Six crops suit Loni Deokar. The right choice depends on your plot's zone. Rice anchors Zone 1 (flood-proof). Mulberry is Zone 2's flood-tolerant alternative to sugarcane. Banana and pomegranate command premium prices in Zone 3.

!!! tip "The opportunity"
    A spatial crop strategy—rice in high-seepage zones, grapes/onion on elevated land—can **turn waterlogging from a liability into a productivity asset** while protecting high-value crops and adapting to dam-driven water variability.
